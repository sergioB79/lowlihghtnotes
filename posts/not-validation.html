Not Validation

Maybe it was never about validation. Maybe it was just fear of death, trying to disguise itself as the need to be seen.

Maybe it was never about validation.

That word sounds too clean, too social — as if what I was looking for lived in other people's eyes. But the truth is quieter than that. And darker.

I think it was always something else.

A kind of unease that sits behind everything — behind the need to say something, to write something, to leave a trace where there was none. Not because someone must see it, but because one day I won't be here to say it again.

It's not applause I fear losing.

It's disappearance.

The strange, silent possibility that one day all of this — the thoughts, the voice, the small storms inside the chest — will simply stop. No echo. No witness. Just the world continuing as if nothing had ever passed through me.

So maybe it was never about validation.

Maybe it was just fear of death, trying to disguise itself as the need to be seen.
