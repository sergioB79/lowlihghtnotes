How to Stay With It

Understanding does not make the world simpler. If anything, it makes it heavier. And yet, something else grows there.

The more we understand, the more capable we become of loving reality as it is.

Not the softened version of it. Not the one we imagine when we are young, where meaning is neat and justice arrives on time. But the real one — uneven, unfinished, full of contradictions that refuse to resolve themselves.

Understanding does not make the world simpler.

If anything, it makes it heavier. You start to see the fractures behind things that once looked solid. You realise how fragile certainty is, how provisional every explanation becomes the moment you look at it long enough.

And yet, strangely, something else grows there.

Because when illusion falls away, what remains is not necessarily despair. Sometimes it is a quieter form of affection — a willingness to accept the world without demanding that it justify itself.

To love reality not because it is perfect, but because it is real.

Perhaps that is what understanding does at its best. It does not save us from the world.

It teaches us how to stay with it.
